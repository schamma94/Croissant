🥐 The Croissant 🥐

The croissant originated in Austria as the "kipferl," a crescent-shaped pastry created in the 17th century to celebrate the victory over the Ottoman Empire at the Battle of Vienna in 1683. It was brought to France in the late 18th century, where it evolved into the modern croissant we know today. The French added more butter and refined the pastry, giving it its signature flaky and buttery texture. While the croissant is now closely associated with French cuisine, its roots can be traced back to Austria, where it was initially created to symbolise the crescent moon on the Ottoman Empire's flag.
